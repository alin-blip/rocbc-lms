# Plagiarism & AI Usage Policy — ROCBC

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective from:** [date]  
**Approved by:** Centre Manager

## 1. Purpose

To maintain academic integrity, define acceptable AI use, and establish penalties for academic misconduct in distance learning programmes.

## 2. Definitions

- **Plagiarism:** Submitting someone else's work as your own, including copying from sources without attribution
- **Self-plagiarism:** Reusing previous work without authorisation
- **AI-assisted:** Work where AI tools were used as a supplement but the learner maintained substantive intellectual contribution
- **AI-generated:** Work substantially created by AI without meaningful human input

## 3. Acceptable AI Use

Learners may use AI tools for:
- Brainstorming ideas
- Grammar and style checking
- Research assistance (identifying sources)
- Translation of non-academic content

## 4. Prohibited AI Use

Learners must NOT:
- Use AI to generate complete or substantial portions of assignments
- Use AI to paraphrase sources without original analysis
- Use AI to complete assessment tasks where critical thinking is assessed
- Submit AI-generated work as their own

## 5. Declaration Requirement

Every submission includes a mandatory AI-use declaration:
- "I have / have not used AI tools for this assignment"
- If yes: declare which tools and how they were used

## 6. Detection

- All submissions are screened with Turnitin/Copyleaks (AI detection enabled)
- Assessors may request oral questioning to verify authenticity
- Suspicious submissions are flagged for IV review

## 7. Penalties

| Offence | First Occurrence | Repeat |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Minor (uncited source) | Refer with resubmission | Fail |
| Moderate (extensive copying) | Fail on assessment | Refer to board |
| Severe (AI-generated entire submission) | Fail course | Expulsion |
| Fabrication of evidence | Fail course | Expulsion |

## 8. Learner Support

ROCBC provides training on academic integrity and proper citation during induction.
